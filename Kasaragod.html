Kasaragod


Land of Gods

Kasaragod, embraced by the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats, offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural splendor. The iconic Bekal Fort, with its stunning coastal vistas, stands as a testament to the region's history and architectural marvel. This district is adorned with pristine beaches like Bekal and Cheruvathur, inviting travelers to relax on golden sands and enjoy breathtaking sunsets.

MOST POPULAR PLACES

Bekal Fort.

Bekal Fort is a historical structure built in the 17th century and has itself become part of an identity of the district. It is situated in Pallikara village of Kasaragod on a headland that spans up to 35 km.It is believed that the fort was originally constructed by rulers of the ancient Kadampa dynasty and the power changed hands over the years to Kolathiri Rajas, Hyder Ali, Tipu Sultan and finally to the British East India Company.

Ranipuram Hills.

Ranipuram Hills is a popular scenic hotpot in Kasaragod district which is situated about 43 km away from Kanhangad. Formerly known as Madathumala, this picturesque stretch of hills stands at a height of 750 m above sea level and is the point of highest altitude in the entire district.Ranipuram comprises of evergreen Shola forests, wildflowers, moors and sprawling meadows .

Valyaparamba Backwater

Valiyaparamba backwaters is a picturesque destination in Kasaragod and is situated 10 km south of Nileswaram. The place gets its name from Valiyaparamba island and is fed by four rivers.Valiyaparamba backwaters is dotted with numerous islands..

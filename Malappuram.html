Malappuram


Land of Hills

Malappuram, nestled amidst the lush Western Ghats, is a mosaic of cultural heritage and natural beauty. Its historic significance is evident in the ancient temples like Thirunavaya and Trikkandiyur, which stand as testaments to the region's religious legacy. The district also boasts the serene Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary, a haven for birdwatchers, and the pristine beaches of Tanur and Padinharekara, perfect for tranquil getaways.

Nilambur Teak Museum.

Nilambur teak is famous for its quality. One of the world's most sought after woods, it has unique features like a large size, durability and its distinctive colour. Nilambur teak is said to possess high resistance to fungal decay and also exhibit antioxidant properties..

Kottakkunnu.

One of the best place in Malappuram district to spend leisure hours. Should reach around 4PM for getting convenient parking place. Best experience when the 'python' like steps are ascended. Those who can't go through steps can park your vehicle at the top. The table top view is marvellous with a lot of rides for children.

Keralamkundu Waterfalls.

Dreaming of a place to take a dip in the cold waters amid lush greenery? Then pack your bags and head to Keralamkundu Waterfalls in the Malappuram district. Located 1,500 feet above sea level in a small village called Karuvarakkundu, Keralamkundu Waterfalls is a sought-after destination in the district.

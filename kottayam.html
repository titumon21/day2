Kottayam


Land of Letters, Latex, and Lakes

Kottayam, known for its literary legacy and natural charm, captivates visitors with its serene backwaters and lush landscapes. The district's historic importance as a center of education and literature is evident in its numerous libraries, publishing houses, and the renowned Mahatma Gandhi University. The backwaters, particularly in Kumarakom, offer enchanting houseboat experiences, while the ancient churches, like the St. Mary's Church in Bharananganam, showcase the area's rich religious heritage.

MOST POPULAR PLACES

Illikkal Kallu.

Three hills, each rising to 4,000 ft and above, together form this huge hill. Each of these hills have a peculiar shape. One of them resembles a mushroom owing to which it gets its name Kuda Kallu (umbrella shaped rock). The medicinal herb Neela Koduveli, which bathes the hillsides in blue, grows here. This flower is also believed to possess supernatural powers which could increase wealth and ensure a rich harvest.

Ilaveezha Poonchira.

Ilaveezha poonchira, a hill station near Melukavu Mattom, 60kms from Kottayam is gaining tourist importance. Located among three beautiful hillocks Mankunnu, Kudayathoormala and Thonippara, Poonchira is a place of picturesque charm. Legends say that the pool here was a bathing place for Panchali the legendary heroine of Mahabharata. .

Aruvikuzhi Waterfalls

Aruvikuzhi is a beautiful picnic spot and an ideal shooting location for movies. Aruvikuzhy Waterfalls, falling in five steps, is a real feast for the eyes. Here streams make their way through the landscape and the water roars as it cascades down the mountains from a height of 100 ft. It is an ideal place for trekking and is located in the midst of rubber plantations.
